Getting costs for contraception paid
In order to help less affluent citizens to be able to decide themselves how they would like to use contraception for family planning, the city of Munich pays the costs for contraception prescribed by a doctor.
Who’s it for:

Citizens who are at least 22 years old, have the München-Pass and/or receive one of the following benefits:
- Help with living costs or Grundsicherung (subsistence) (SGB XII)
- Arbeitslosengeld II (unemployment benefit II) or Sozialgeld (income support) (SGB II)
- Benefits under the Asylbewerberleistungsgesetz or AsylbLG (Benefits for Asylum Seekers Act)
- Wohngeld (housing allowance) or Kinderzuschlag (child benefit supplement)
People with low income (except au-pairs, apprentices, students). The income limit is checked individually.
Participants in the Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr (voluntary social year) or Ökologisches Jahr (ecological year), volunteers in the federal voluntary service (Bundesfreiwilligendienst)

Documents required:
München-Pass OR approval letter regarding proof of income, assets and rent, proof of social or ecological year or federal voluntary service
Where to apply:
at the relevant Sozialbürgerhaus or at the Amt für Wohnen und Migration (if receiving benefits according to the Benefits for Asylum Seekers Act).
