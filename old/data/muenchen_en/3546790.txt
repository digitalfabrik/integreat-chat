München-Pass
The  München-Pass offers residents of Munich with low income many discounts, for example when using the indoor and outdoor swimming pools, cultural institutions and sport clubs. The pass also gives you discounts in the MVV regional public transport network: You receive reduced price day tickets at the counters of the Sozialbürgerhäuser and the Amt für Wohnen und Migration. The monthly ticket IsarCard S-Sozialticket is available at all MVV sales points.
 https://www.muenchen.de/muenchen-pass
Who’s it for:

Munich residents (and their family members) who get one of the following benefits:- Help with living costs or Grundsicherung (subsistence) (SGB XII)- Arbeitslosengeld II (unemployment benefit II) (SGB II)- Benefits under the Asylbewerberleistungsgesetz or AsylbLG (Benefits for Asylum Seekers Act)- Wohngeld (housing allowance) or Kinderzuschlag (child benefit supplement)
People with low income (except au-pairs, apprentices, students). The income limit is checked individually.
Participants in the Freiwilliges Soziales Jahr (voluntary social year) or Ökologisches Jahr (ecological year), volunteers in the federal voluntary service (Bundesfreiwilligendienst)

Documents required:

Payment approval letter and/or documents proving income, property and rent
Passport photo (one from every family member who is eligible, because every family member receives their own pass)
Document about the voluntary social or ecological year or federal voluntary service

How to apply:

from your relevant claims administrator in the Sozialbürgerhaus, if you receive help with living costs or Grundsicherung (subsistence)
from the administrator for voluntary services in the relevant Sozialbürgerhaus (if you receive unemployment benefit II, housing allowance or child benefit supplement); except participants in the voluntary year (FSJ, FEJ) and volunteers in the programme of the federal voluntary service (Bundesfreiwilligendienst)
at the Amt für Wohnen und Migration (if receiving benefits according to the asylum-seekers benefits law and if you are homeless)
