
Labour exploitation
Labour exploitation means: when unfair conditions prevail at work or the labour is exploited without the consent of the person concerned. Trafficked persons receive no or too little money for their work. They have to work under bad or even dangerous conditions. They are threatened or experience violence.

Slavery and forced labour are prohibited in Germany.
Human trafficking or forced sex work is prohibited in Germany.

Other forms of exploitation are also punishable:

No wage payment even though you have worked.
Working longer hours for the same wage than agreed in the employment contract (circumventing the minimum wage).
No payment of wages due to you, such as sick pay or paid holidays.
