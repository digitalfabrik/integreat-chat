Permanent residence
Niederlassungserlaubnis (settlement permit) according to Section 9 Aufenthaltsgesetz
With the Niederlassungserlaubnis (settlement permit), you can live and work in Germany without time or location limits.
Requirements (selected):

Legal residence in Germany for at least five years (earlier study and training times are calculated at 50 percent)
Valid residence permit that was not issued for a temporary purpose (study) or for humanitarian reasons
Proof of secure living costs and housing
Proof of sufficient retirement planning (at least 60 months obligatory contributions or voluntary contributions to state pension insurance in Germany)
Sufficient German language knowledge and knowledge about the legal & societal rules and living conditions in Germany – usually proved by:


Confirmation that you have taken part in an integration course

or

Study certificates and references about completed job training in Germany or other language certificates

Further documents may be required in individual cases.
You can find more information on these requirements  here.

Permanent residence – EU
The permission for permanent residence – EU (Daueraufenthalt – EU) is a permanent residence permit and offers the same rights as the Niederlassungserlaubnis (settlement permit). With the permission for permanent residence, you can live and work in Germany without time or location limits.
Furthermore, you can fundamentally also live, work or study in the countries of the European Union (with the exception of Ireland, Denmark, Great Britain), (special requirements apply for individual countries).
You can find more information on these requirements  here.
Whether a Niederlassungserlaubnis (settlement permit) or an Erlaubnis zum Daueraufenthalt – EU (permission for permanent residence) is better for you depends on your individual circumstances. The »» Ausländerbehörde (Immigration Office) would be happy to advise you.
